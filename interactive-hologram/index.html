Meet Sonia

The life, courage, and legacy behind the experience.

Sonia Warshawski’s story is both devastating and deeply human. Her testimony continues to educate and move audiences across generations.

Sonia Warshawski
Preserving testimony through technology

Early Life

Sonia Warshawski, née Grynstein, was born on November 10, 1925, in Miezric, Poland. She was 13 when war began and only 15 when Nazis forced the Jews of her town into the Miezric Ghetto.

The Holocaust

Sonia was imprisoned in Auschwitz, Majdanek, and Bergen-Belsen. She witnessed the murder of her mother and survived some of the most notorious death camps of the Holocaust. She was liberated by British troops at age 19.

A Life of Testimony

After immigrating to the United States in 1948 and settling in Kansas City, Kansas, Sonia later became known for sharing her story with anyone willing to listen. Her tailor shop became a place of extraordinary human connection. Her voice reached schools, museums, synagogues, churches, theaters, and even prisons.

Continuing Her Legacy

Sonia’s life also reached many through the documentary Big Sonia. The Blue Card is honored to continue sharing her testimony through this interactive hologram program so new audiences can learn directly from her story with dignity, depth, and immediacy.
